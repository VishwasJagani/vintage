About Us

The Vintage Autohaus was established in 1962 as a community project to promote the history of the automotive industry and to promote tourism in the area. At the time, a number of prominent residents made financial contributions or provided interest-free loans to acquire a former car dealership. It was converted into a facility where antique automobiles and related artifacts could be displayed. The Vintage Autohaus opened in 1963 and was incorporated in October 1964 as a charitable, not-for-profit institution.

Over 50,000 square feet of Exhibit Space

Today the Vintage Autohaus continues to maintain the nearly century-old former car dealership building at 99 Simcoe St. South. The main floor holds the majority of vehicles, while the second floor tells the story of automotive journey throughout the World. A gift shop and reference library compliment the automobiles and the adjoining lot hosts drive-ins and car shows.

Accessible Parking

Guests may be dropped off at the entrance on the first floor. Accessible parking spots (19) are only located on the first floor of the parking garage (P1), close to the main entrance. There are no accessible parking spaces on the second, third, or fourth levels. The second, third, and fourth levels of the parking garage are not serviced by elevators.
